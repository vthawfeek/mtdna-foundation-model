Building an mtDNA foundation model on my laptop. Before writing any model code, I checked whether existing models could handle this. They can't.

---
DNABERT2 encodes positions 1 and 16,569 of mitochondrial DNA as 16,568 steps apart. They share a phosphodiester bond. That's the circular junction problem in one number.
---
The D-loop control region wraps across that junction. Both mtDNA promoters and the origin of replication are there. With linear positional encoding, attention heads modeling D-loop coherence must attend across the full sequence length. The region looks discontinuous.
---
The heteroplasmy gap: DNABERT2, HyenaDNA, and Nucleotide Transformer all accept one sequence per input. No per-position float channel. You cannot represent a 73% heteroplasmy level at position 3,243. The interface doesn't exist.
---
Fine-tuning on mtDNA data doesn't fix either problem. Positional encoding is structural, not learned from the training distribution. A missing input channel cannot be added by fine-tuning.
---
Getting the D-loop right needs circular PE baked in. Heteroplasmy needs a new input dimension. Neither comes from fine-tuning. Building from scratch.
