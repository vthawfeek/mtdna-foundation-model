Reading pathogenicity from the wrong place in a transformer gives you worse results.

For variant classification, CLS is a global summary of the whole sequence window. But whether a mutation causes disease depends on what it does at *that position*, in *that gene*. CLS dilutes exactly the signal you need.
---
The fix: extract the hidden state at the token covering the mutation site.

variant_hidden = H[batch_idx, variant_token_idx, :]

512-token window centered on the variant → local context only. Position matters.
---
Dataset construction: 2k ClinVar pathogenic mtDNA variants (positive) + 5k gnomAD common variants AF > 0.01 (negative).

pos_weight=2.5 in BCEWithLogitsLoss. Missing a real pathogenic variant costs more than a false positive. The loss function reflects that.
---
LoRA r=4 here vs r=8 for haplogroup (Day 16).

Reason: 7k variant windows vs 47k full genomes. Smaller dataset = smaller adapter capacity. weight_decay=0.1 instead of 0.01 for the same reason.
---
Building the first dedicated mtDNA foundation model for pathogenic variant detection, haplogroup classification, and heteroplasmy prediction. The full genome is 16,569 bp and laptop-trainable.
