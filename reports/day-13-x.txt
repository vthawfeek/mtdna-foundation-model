Before a single training step, the mtDNA-FM model correctly classifies 9.5% of sequences by haplogroup (vs 4% random). That's not learning. Here's what it actually means:
---
The signal is in the k-mer content of the first 128bp. Haplogroup-defining variants in the D-loop create consistent 6-mer differences between clades. Even random embeddings of those k-mer IDs cluster somewhat by haplogroup.
---
Cosine similarity k-NN: 9.5%. Euclidean k-NN: 29.5%. The difference is all artefact. Euclidean picks up embedding magnitude differences caused by which k-mers appear — not semantic similarity. Cosine removes the back door.
---
Sanity check for any MLM: initial loss should equal ln(vocabulary size). mtDNA-FM has 4,102 tokens. Expected: 8.320. Measured at step 5: 8.322. Implementation correct.
---
Attention at step 0 is structureless — uniform across all 8 heads × 6 layers. That's the expected baseline. Structured local context (diagonal attention, D-loop motifs) should emerge by step 25k. Heatmaps will be updated once Phase 1 completes.
