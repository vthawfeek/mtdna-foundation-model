---
The pathogenicity model is built and trained. I have no way to evaluate whether it learned anything.
---
The architecture: extract the hidden state at the variant's position in the encoder output (not CLS, not mean pool). Project through a linear head. LoRA r=4 fine-tuning on top of the pre-trained encoder. Pathogenicity is local; you want the local hidden state.
---
What the evaluation would need: ClinVar pathogenic mtDNA variants as positives. gnomAD common variants (AF > 0.01) as benign controls. A clean train/test split stratified by variant type (missense, tRNA, rRNA).
---
Why it doesn't exist: the data engineering to join, clean, and balance ClinVar + gnomAD takes a full day. That time was spent building the training pipeline instead. Wrong order.
---
What's honest: the architecture is complete. The weights are on HuggingFace. The performance is unknown. "It runs" is not the same as "it works."
