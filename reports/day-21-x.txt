The haplogroup adapter for mtDNA-FM is 400 KB. The base model is 44.6 MB. Downstream tasks share the base, each adapter costs less than 1% extra storage. Both are now live on HuggingFace Hub.
---
What "live on Hub" actually required: the original push script was uploading fresh random-weight adapters instead of the trained ones. PEFT's save_pretrained doesn't validate whether weights are meaningful — it just saves whatever you give it.
---
The fix is straightforward once you see it: copy the trained adapter files directly, then patch adapter_config.json to replace the local path with the Hub model ID. Without that patch, any user who loads the adapter outside your filesystem gets a file-not-found error.
---
Zero-shot haplogroup accuracy: 50% vs 12.5% random (8-class sampled subset). The model was never explicitly trained to classify haplogroups — it learned phylogenetic structure from sequence alone during pre-training on ~117k vertebrate mtDNA genomes.
---
