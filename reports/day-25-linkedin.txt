A pre-trained mtDNA model with no haplogroup labels during training produces embeddings where related haplogroups cluster together in 2-D. L-clade (African root), H/HV (European), and M-clade (East Asian) sequences separate visibly. The silhouette score confirms the separation is real in the full 256-d space, not just a t-SNE artefact.

This is Day 25 of building the first foundation model for mitochondrial DNA, a molecule whose mutations drive over 350 inherited diseases including MELAS, Leigh syndrome, and Leber hereditary optic neuropathy.

What the showcase notebook tests is not what the model was trained to do, but what it learned without being asked. Beyond the t-SNE, three results stand out: the haplogroup confusion errors concentrate between phylogenetically adjacent clades (L0/L1, H/HV), never between distant branches; pathogenicity prediction reaches AUROC 0.877, up from a k-mer baseline of 0.720; and Neanderthal and Denisovan embeddings land outside modern human diversity, near the phylogenetic root, consistent with paleoanthropological consensus and with no fine-tuning.
