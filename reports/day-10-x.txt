Before training starts, you already know what the loss will be.

For a 4,102-token k-mer vocabulary, the theoretical random baseline MLM loss is log(4,102) = 8.32.

Smoke test confirmed: initial loss = 8.3244. Now the training can begin.
---
Gradient accumulation is an arithmetic identity, not an approximation.

16 sequences × 8 micro-steps = 128 effective batch. Memory cost of 16, gradient quality of 128.

The math: sum 8 micro-gradients before stepping = identical to gradient of one 128-sample batch.
---
Phase 2 of pre-training cannot resume Phase 1's optimizer.

AdamW stores per-parameter moment estimates calibrated to 50k steps of cross-species gradients.

Resuming them on human mtDNA data = wrong curvature correction at the worst possible moment.
---
The fix: load only encoder weights (keys starting with "mtdna."), discard the rest.

Prediction heads reinitialize fresh. Optimizer starts clean. Phase 2 adapts correctly.

This is the standard domain-adaptive pre-training pattern, but it's easy to get wrong.
