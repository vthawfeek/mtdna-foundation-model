Standard BERT positional encoding treats position 1 and position 16,569 of mitochondrial DNA as 16,568 apart. They share a phosphodiester bond. Here's why that breaks, and the fix:
---
mtDNA is circular. The D-loop control region wraps around the 16569/1 junction. Any attention head modeling promoter structure is working with corrupted positional signals if the PE is linear. The circular fix replaces pos/10000^(...) with (2pi*pos/16569)/10000^(...).
---
The second problem: heteroplasmy. The m.3243A>G MELAS mutation is asymptomatic at 30% and causes encephalomyopathy above 70%. Standard DNA models accept one base per position. mtDNA-FM accepts a continuous het_level per position, projected into embedding space alongside the k-mer ID.
